üìö Fascinating Japan Facts

üóª

Mount Fuji

Mount Fuji is Japan's highest mountain at 3,776 meters. It last erupted in 1707 and is considered one of Japan's "Three Holy Mountains."

üöÑ

Shinkansen

Japan's bullet trains reach speeds of 320 km/h. The network has carried over 10 billion passengers with a perfect safety record!

üå∏

Cherry Blossoms

Hanami (cherry blossom viewing) has been celebrated for over 1,000 years. There are over 200 varieties of cherry trees in Japan!

üç£

Sushi Heritage

Sushi was originally a way to preserve fish in fermented rice. The modern nigiri-style sushi was invented in Tokyo in the 1800s.

‚õ©Ô∏è

Shrines & Temples

Japan has over 80,000 Shinto shrines and 77,000 Buddhist temples. Kyoto alone has 1,600 temples and 400 shrines!

üéå

Islands

Japan consists of 6,852 islands! The four main islands (Honshu, Hokkaido, Kyushu, and Shikoku) make up 97% of the land area.

üí° Did You Know?

Culture

In Japan, it's customary to slurp your noodles loudly - it shows appreciation and enhances the flavor!

Technology

Japan has more than 5 million vending machines - that's one for every 23 people! You can buy almost anything from them.

Nature

Snow monkeys in Nagano are the only primates (other than humans) that enjoy bathing in hot springs!

Food

Square watermelons are grown in Japan! They're easier to stack and store, but cost around $100-200 each.

Entertainment

Karaoke was invented in Japan in 1971! The word comes from "kara" (empty) and "okesutora" (orchestra).

Architecture

The H≈çry≈´-ji temple in Nara is the world's oldest surviving wooden structure, built in 607 AD!

Transportation

Tokyo's train system is so punctual that the average delay is just 18 seconds! They apologize for delays over 1 minute.

Tradition

There are professional "pushers" (oshiya) hired to pack people into crowded trains during rush hour!
